Abstract

Multi-view diffusion models have recently emerged as a powerful paradigm for novel view synthesis, yet the underlying mechanism that enables their view consistency remains unclear. In this work, we first verify that the Inflated 3D Attention Maps of these models naturally acquire geometric correspondence throughout training. However, this correspondence signal remains incomplete and fragile, with its accuracy degrading significantly under large viewpoint changes.

Building on these findings, we introduce CAMEO, a training technique that directly supervises attention maps using a Geometric Correspondence Map derived from pointmaps. We demonstrate that supervising a single attention layer (specifically Layer 10) is sufficient to guide the model toward learning precise correspondences. CAMEO reduces the number of training iterations required for convergence by half while achieving superior performance in preserving geometry and structure compared to baselines.
